About OperationsEdge

How We Review. Why It Matters.

The editorial methodology behind our reviews and comparisons — what we test, how we score, and how we handle commercial relationships.

Who we are

OperationsEdge is an independent review and resource site for UK operations managers, warehouse managers, and logistics professionals. We cover the SaaS tools that operations teams actually use — warehouse management systems, workforce scheduling platforms, H&S audit tools, project management software, and more.

Our content is written by people with direct experience managing UK warehouse and logistics operations. Not tech journalists. Not generalist reviewers. People who have dealt with the reality of UK compliance obligations, frontline workforces, multi-site distribution, and the specific constraints of British operations environments.

The site was founded because the software review landscape is full of content that has no operational grounding — written by people who have never run a pick-face, never dealt with a HSE improvement notice, and never had to explain why the WMS integration failed during peak. We're trying to fix that.

What we cover

We publish five types of content:

  • Reviews — detailed, scored assessments of individual tools
  • Comparisons — head-to-head evaluations of two tools for a specific use case
  • Guides — practical buyer's guides and how-to content for operations professionals
  • From the Floor — first-person case studies from UK operations practitioners
  • Resources — free templates, checklists, and reference tools

We focus on tools that are relevant for UK operations — which means we take UK pricing (in GBP where possible), UK compliance obligations (HMRC, HSE, UK GDPR, WTR), UK support hours, and UK-specific integrations seriously. A tool that's great for US logistics teams but awkward for UK payroll or RIDDOR reporting is not a great tool for our readers.

Our review methodology

1

Tool selection

We choose tools to review based on relevance to UK operations teams, search volume (what people are actually trying to find), reader requests, and — where applicable — whether a tool has an affiliate programme we can apply to. We publish this openly in our disclosure policy. We do not publish only tools with affiliate programmes, and affiliate status does not determine which tools we recommend.

2

Hands-on evaluation

We sign up for free trials and, where possible, use the tool in real operational contexts. We use the mobile app, try to build automations, test integrations, and go through the support process. Where we haven't personally used a tool, we draw on documented experience from UK operations practitioners and are transparent about the basis of our assessment.

3

Structured scoring

Every scored review uses the same criteria framework, tailored to the tool category. For workforce scheduling tools, we score ease of use, shift pattern support, WTR compliance features, mobile experience, and value for money. For H&S platforms, we score audit features, action management, mobile usability, reporting, and cost. Category-specific criteria are stated in each review.

4

Pricing verification

We verify pricing at the time of publication and flag the verification date in the article metadata. SaaS pricing changes frequently. If you find pricing that differs from what's in a review, please let us know and we'll update it.

5

Publishing and updating

Reviews are published with a date and updated when pricing, features, or our assessment changes materially. We don't quietly update content without flagging it — the "Updated" date in the article metadata reflects when a meaningful change was made.

Editorial independence

Our editorial opinions are not for sale. Vendors cannot pay to receive a positive review, a higher score, or a "Best Pick" designation. We have never published a paid review and will not do so.

We do participate in affiliate programmes, which means we may earn a commission if you purchase a tool through a link on our site. This is disclosed clearly in every article where it applies, and we maintain a full affiliate disclosure page. Affiliate income does not influence our scores or verdicts — tools we recommend are tools we believe are genuinely appropriate for UK operations teams, regardless of whether they have an affiliate programme.

Where a tool doesn't have an affiliate programme, we still review it if we think it's relevant and worth covering. Where we have concerns about a tool — cost, missing features, poor UK support — we say so plainly.

Our editorial principles

Honest over positive

We'd rather flag a genuine weakness than protect a vendor relationship. Every review includes a "Where it falls short" section because no tool is perfect for every operation.

UK-specific over generic

UK pricing, UK compliance, UK support hours, UK payroll integration. We flag when a tool is US-built and the UK experience is secondary.

Practical over theoretical

We focus on what the tool is actually like to use in a real warehouse or logistics operation, not what the product page says it can do.

Transparent about relationships

We disclose affiliate relationships clearly and consistently. If we earn a commission from a link, you'll know about it.

Current, not historical

Pricing and features change. We maintain reviews over time and flag when information is out of date. If something has changed, tell us.

Ops experience, not tech journalism

Our reviewers have managed warehouse and logistics operations. We write from that perspective, not from a generic software-evaluation one.
