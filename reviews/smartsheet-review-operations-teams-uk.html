Smartsheet for Operations Teams: Honest Review

Smartsheet is powerful, data-heavy, and enterprise-grade. But is that what your operations team actually needs? Here's the unvarnished view from a UK ops perspective.

Smartsheet is one of the most capable work management platforms available — but it's not for every operations team. It's spreadsheet-first, data-heavy, and built with enterprise-scale complexity in mind. For the right team, it's genuinely excellent. For most day-to-day warehouse operations, it's likely more tool than you need.

The honest question isn't "is Smartsheet good?" — it is. The question is whether it's the right fit for the way your team actually works and the problems you're actually trying to solve.

What is Smartsheet?

Smartsheet is a cloud-based work management and collaboration platform. Its core interface is a grid that looks and behaves like Microsoft Excel — with rows, columns, and formulas you'd recognise — but layered with project management features: Gantt charts, automated workflows, cross-sheet reporting, dashboards, and approval processes.

It's not a WMS. It's not an ERP. It doesn't manage stock levels, connect to barcode scanners, or generate despatch labels. What it does is give operations teams a structured, scalable environment for managing complex projects, programmes, and multi-site activity — with the data rigour that a large operation genuinely needs.

Smartsheet is particularly strong in environments where teams are already living in spreadsheets and need something that feels familiar but goes further — more accountability, more automation, better cross-team visibility, and proper audit trails.

Where Smartsheet works well for operations teams

Complex project and programme management

If your operations team is running capital projects, site expansions, equipment installations, or compliance programmes, Smartsheet is genuinely built for this. Critical path analysis, baseline tracking, predecessor dependencies, and Gantt chart exports are all first-class features. You can manage a six-month site fit-out across multiple workstreams with real confidence in the data.

For project-heavy operations — contract logistics sites, new site ramp-ups, or multi-phase improvement programmes — Smartsheet handles complexity that simpler tools buckle under.

Cross-site reporting and roll-up dashboards

One of Smartsheet's genuine strengths is its ability to aggregate data across multiple sheets into roll-up reports and dashboards. If you're managing multiple sites or depots and need a consolidated view of KPIs, incident counts, open actions, or SLA performance, Smartsheet handles this considerably better than most alternatives.

The cross-sheet formula capability is materially more powerful than Monday.com. If you're building anything resembling a data model — with lookups, conditional logic, and multi-source aggregations — Smartsheet is closer to Excel than any other work management tool in this category.

Microsoft 365-heavy environments

For operations teams already embedded in the Microsoft ecosystem, Smartsheet's native integrations are a significant advantage. It connects directly with Teams, SharePoint, and Power BI — meaning you can embed live Smartsheet data into your existing reporting infrastructure rather than building a parallel system.

If your site uses Teams for daily communication and SharePoint for document management, Smartsheet slots in more naturally than competitors. Monday.com integrates with Microsoft tools, but Smartsheet's connection is deeper and more tightly maintained.

Compliance and governed operations

Regulated industries — pharmaceutical distribution, medical device logistics, food-grade warehousing — often need more formal process controls than general-purpose work management tools offer. Smartsheet's approval workflows, digital sign-offs, locked baselines, and detailed audit logs make it a credible choice for operations where compliance records genuinely matter.

If your site is subject to BRC, GMP, or ISO audits and you need to demonstrate documented process adherence, Smartsheet's governance features give you something to show auditors. That's a specific but real use case that not every platform handles well.

Large teams and resource management

Smartsheet's resource management module — available on Business and Enterprise plans — allows you to manage capacity across large teams and multiple projects. For operations managers responsible for 50+ headcount across complex shift patterns and project activity, having a resource view that ties into project timelines is genuinely useful.

This isn't relevant for most warehouse operations, but if you're running a shared-service operations function or managing project resources across sites, it fills a gap that most competitors don't.

Where Smartsheet falls short

The limitations matter, and they're worth being direct about:

  • Steeper learning curve — Smartsheet is intuitive if you're Excel-fluent. If your team isn't, the grid interface can feel cold and unfamiliar compared to Monday.com's visual boards. Expect a longer onboarding period and more training investment.
  • No permanent free plan — unlike Monday.com, Smartsheet has no free tier. You get a 30-day trial, after which you're paying from day one. For small operations teams exploring options, this raises the cost of experimentation.
  • Weaker mobile experience — Smartsheet has iOS and Android apps, but they're less polished and capable than Monday.com's. For supervisors who need to update tasks or check dashboards from the floor, this is a meaningful limitation.
  • Pricing escalates quickly — the entry Pro plan (~£9/user/month) is comparable to Monday.com, but the Business tier (where the genuinely useful features like resource management and advanced reports live) jumps to ~£19/user/month. For a 20-person team, that's a noticeable step up.
  • Overkill for simple ops — if you need task boards, inbound tracking, daily briefings, and basic KPI dashboards, Smartsheet is more complexity than the use case justifies. The onboarding overhead alone will slow you down in the short term.
  • Not a WMS — Smartsheet will not manage stock levels, pick paths, or barcode scanning. If that's the gap you're trying to fill, this is not the answer.
"Smartsheet is not competing with your WMS. It's competing with the layer of spreadsheets, email chains, and manual reports sitting above your WMS — and at that layer, for complex operations, it's very good."

Verdict: Is Smartsheet right for your operations team?

Here's an honest breakdown by team type:

  • Use it If: You're running complex capital projects, multi-site programmes, or compliance-heavy operations and need formal project controls, audit trails, and cross-sheet data management.
  • Use it If: Your team is deeply embedded in Microsoft 365 and you want a work management tool that connects natively with Teams, SharePoint, and Power BI.
  • Consider it alongside Monday.com if: You need both the ease-of-use of visual boards for daily task management and the reporting power of Smartsheet for programme oversight — some larger ops teams run both.
  • Skip it If: You need a tool for day-to-day warehouse task management, inbound tracking, or basic KPI dashboards. Monday.com will get you there faster and cheaper.
  • Skip it If: Your team isn't Excel-fluent and you need fast adoption. The learning curve will cost you more than the tool saves in the short term.
