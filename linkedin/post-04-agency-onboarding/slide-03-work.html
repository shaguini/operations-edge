The Planning Chain 3 / 5

Four things you have
to get right before day one.

1
Contact multiple agencies — compare rates, availability, and operator profile

Cheapest isn't always lowest risk. We looked at reliability, sector experience, and typical notice period compliance.

2
Compare agency cost against budget AND the risk of under-resourcing

Not just "can we afford this?" but "what does failing to cover this peak actually cost us?"

3
Assign buddy operators before starters arrive

Named individuals. Briefed that their own output may dip during coaching. Given an escalation route if a starter isn't progressing.

4
Define a structured ramp-up: controlled tasks → independence → full allocation

Not "induction then straight to the floor." A deliberate progression with readiness checkpoints.
