We needed a group of agency starters on the floor within a short window.

The recruitment part was straightforward. The onboarding part is where most operations quietly fall apart.

People arrive. They get a brief induction. They go straight to the floor. The permanent team ends up babysitting instead of picking. And you're paying agency rates for headcount that isn't contributing yet.

We did it differently. Before a single person arrived, we had the ramp-up planned: controlled tasks first, named buddy operators briefed, readiness checkpoints defined, escalation routes agreed.

Within the first week, they were on full allocation. The permanent team's output didn't drop.
