Most warehouse induction checklists are designed to produce a signed form.

That form means someone was in the room. It doesn't mean they know what to do when the fire alarm goes off, or who to talk to when something isn't right.

When we planned the onboarding of a group of agency starters, we stripped induction back to five things. No extras. No filler. Just the five things that, if missing, create an incident, a confusion, or a failure on day one.

Walk them to the emergency exit — don't point. Get a dated, witnessed manual handling sign-off. Confirm the PPE fits before they enter the operation. Get written acknowledgement on site rules. Give them a name, not "speak to a supervisor."

Five things. Every starter. No exceptions.
