When we started mapping picking errors with a fishbone diagram, something uncomfortable came out.

People — operator behaviour, habits, training gaps — were a contributing factor in about 30% of errors.

Process and Environment were contributing factors in over 70%.

We were running an operation that was generating its own errors and calling them operator mistakes.

The fishbone has five categories: People, Process, Equipment, Environment, Materials. The point is to map every recurring error across all five before deciding what to fix. Otherwise, you're guessing. And the guess is almost always "retrain the team."

Retraining a team for errors caused by poor pick face lighting doesn't reduce errors. It just makes people feel blamed.
