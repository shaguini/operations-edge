Every warehouse has a return-to-work process.

Most run it as a series of individual conversations — one absence at a time, one interview at a time.

That's correct procedure. It's also blind to the shape of the problem.

We added three things to what we already did:

1. Monthly pattern review — instances by person, not just total days
2. Day-of-week mapping — not as evidence, as a question
3. Bradford Factor scores shared openly with the team

The most useful thing wasn't any of these individually. It was seeing the data across the whole team, in one view, on a regular cadence.

When we mapped absences by day of week, Monday and Friday accounted for 55% of all instances across the team.

The conversation that followed wasn't about those days. It was about what sits either side of rest days — and whether shift allocation was contributing to the pattern.

You can manage every absence and never manage absence.

When did you last look at your absence data as a pattern rather than a case list?
