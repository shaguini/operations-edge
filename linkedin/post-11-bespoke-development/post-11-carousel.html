The Problem 2 / 5

A generic plan
would have failed both

The KPI scorecard flagged two team leaders with performance gaps. On paper, both looked like a training problem. In practice, they were completely different situations — and diagnosing that difference was the whole job.

What the data showed

  • One leader: strong technically, but people process compliance was slipping and communication style was creating friction in the team
  • Other leader: excellent communicator and natural leader, but lacking technical confidence in a specific, complex part of the operation
  • Neither gap would have been fixed by the same module at the same time

The scorecard shows you where the gap is. Understanding the person shows you why. The intervention comes from combining both — not just reacting to the metric.

The Approach 3 / 5

Two leaders.
Two completely different interventions.

Experienced leader

Behavioural gap

Directive coaching

GROW + SBI feedback framework

  • Coaching focused on reflection, not instruction — encouraging ownership of the change
  • Feedback tied to observable impact on others, not intent or personality
  • Mentored through proximity: issues addressed in the moment, in context
  • Bi-weekly reviews gated on metric improvement before progressing

High direction · High challenge

New leader

Technical gap

Supportive mentoring

Enablement strategy — no correction needed

  • Shift plan reviewed together before each shift — then she ran it alone
  • Coaching questions rather than instructions: "where do you see the pinch points?"
  • Autonomy protected — no micromanaging of her communication style
  • Safety net visible; used rarely

Low direction · High support
