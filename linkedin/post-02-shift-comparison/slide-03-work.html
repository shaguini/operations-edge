What We Found 3 / 5

Three conditions
that explained the gap.

1
Consistent station allocation

Late shift operators worked the same zones most nights. Familiarity reduced errors and wasted movement. Early shift rotated more frequently.

2
Fewer management interruptions during peak pick windows

Late shift ran leaner on management presence. Operators got into flow states. Early shift had more ad-hoc check-ins breaking concentration.

3
Stronger peer accountability

Late shift had a tighter team culture. Slower pickers were noticed and supported by colleagues — not waiting for a manager to flag it.
