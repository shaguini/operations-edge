Our late shift consistently outperformed our early shift. Every week, for months.

The default assumption would be: better people on late. Wrong.

Both shifts had similar operator profiles. When we broke it down, the gap came down to three things: consistent station allocation, fewer interruptions during pick windows, and stronger peer accountability.

Conditions — not individuals.

When we applied those same conditions to the early shift, the gap closed within weeks. No retraining. No restructure.
