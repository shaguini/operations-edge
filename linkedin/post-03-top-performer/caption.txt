One operator in our picking team was consistently significantly above the team average.

In most warehouses, they get a "well done" and a mention in a team brief. We treated this operator like a process to be documented.

We watched them work. We mapped what they did differently. Three things stood out: pick path discipline, controlled scanner rhythm, and proactive replenishment timing.

None of it was talent. All of it was replicable.

We built those behaviours into how the rest of the team was briefed and coached. The gap didn't disappear — but most of it closed.
