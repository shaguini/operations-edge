Every warehouse has a shift handover process.

Most of them don't work.

Not because people aren't filling them in — they are. The sheet gets written. The template gets used. And then the incoming shift manager spends the first hour firefighting things that were documented three hours ago.

We fixed three things:

1. Priority block at the top — one line per item, maximum three
2. Status on every outstanding task — not started / in progress / needs day team
3. A five-minute handover walk — both managers, same time, structured not conversational

The firefighting didn't stop immediately.

But we could see when it had — because first-hour escalations dropped, and "I didn't know about that" stopped being the most common phrase in the morning debrief.

The document wasn't the problem. The design of the document was.
